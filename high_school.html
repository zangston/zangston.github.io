Herndon High School

"Winston, I may be more educated than you, but I sure as hell am not more intelligent than you." - Dr. Jim Hull, Ph.D.

Located on Bennet Street, just outside of the official borders of Herndon, and across the street from my neighborhood, Herndon High School boasts a reputation of being one of the most diverse high schools in the highest funded school district in America. Recently renovated thanks to the taxpayers of Herndon, HHS has expanded its facilities, including a much larger library, as well as a new "one-to-one" program, providing each student with a laptop to use both at school and at home. Hernon High has a robust foreign language program, as well as accelerated mathematics, and sports, having produced several D1 collegiate athletes. Herndon High School has had the pleasure of playing at the D-Day 75th Anniversary parade in Paris, with some of its students appearing on national televsion to give a speech next to US President Donald Trump, French President Emmanuel Macron, and UK Prime Minister Theresa May.

The following content on this page chronicle my four years at HHS, and what I've learned.

Freshman Year

I came to Herndon High School having already taken Honors Algebra I and Honors Geometry, making me two years ahead in mathematics compared to my peers. My first year of high school involved reiterating on the topics learned in middle school, such as applying the principles of albegra learned in middle school to more advanced equation and functions in Algebra II. I chose to take Programming as an elective course in preparation for computer science, and chose German because I was aware of the exchange program we had with a school in Germany, and wanted to make some international friends.

Class Period Course Description
1 Programming Principles of game design, basic programming skills, learned through use of a graphical interface language
2 German I Introduction to the German language; basic sentence structure and conjugation (normative, accusative cases)
3 Physical Education 9 Topics relating to phys. ed., health, sex ed.
5 Honors World History World history from the first hominids to the Renaissance, intended as a pipeline course to AP World
6 Honors English 9 Analysis of classical literature; topics in writing informative and persuasive papers, as well as citation styles
7 Honors Algebra II Algebraic skills; study of conical, logarithmic, and parabolic functions
8 Honors Biology Topics on ecosystems, cell biology, and basic chemistry; Laboratory experiments involving cultivation, dissection

Sophomore Year

Sophomore year was when I began to take more advanced classes, with one AP course, and the rest Pre-AP. I moved from programming in a graphical interface such as Scratch to programming in Java in an IDE. Sophomore year was also the year I began to take part in various clubs (listed at the bottom of the page), as well as the year I learned to drive.

Class Period Course Description
1 Phys. Ed. 10, & Driver's Ed. Continuation of phys. ed. curriculum, as well as classroom component of driver's education required by the State of Virignia to recieve a student driver's license
2 German II Intermediate sentence structure; past imperfect and past perfect tense; exhange program with Geschwester-Scholl Gymnasium from Freiberg, Germany
3 Computer Science Introduction to programming and computation in Java; course intended to be taken as preparation for AP Computer Science A
5 AP World History (5 on AP Exam) World events from the beginning of mankind up until the present day
6 Honors English 10 Literary analysis in context of women, other cultures, and various marginalized groups
7 Honors Chemistry Principles of stoichiometry, mathematic skills in the context of lab sciences; introduction to the periodic table, ionic and covalent compounds
8 Honors Pre-Calculus w/ Trigonometry Study of parametric equations, trigonometry, and limits

Junior Year

In Junior year, I went all out with the AP courses, having taken 5 AP courses, with two advanced electives. I furthered my programming skillset, having taken a second year of Computer Science, and also Advanced Programming, learning how to make more sophisticated methods in Java and C#, as well as using the Unity engine. I began to explore advanced topics at the collegiate level, such as calculus, history, and chemistry. I began to take on leadership roles as well, having been involved with several extracurricular activities for two years at that point in time.

Class Period Course Description
1 AP Chemistry (4 on AP Exam) Study of chemical compounds, reactions, and laboratory procedures
2 Advanced Programming Programming and game design skills in Unity and C#
3 AP English Language and Composition (5 on AP Exam) Writing informative, argumentative, and analytical papers
5 AP United States History (5 on AP Exam) Study of US history from age of exploration up to present day
6 AP Computer Science A (4 on AP Exam) Programming skills in Java; basic theoretical skills such as time complexity
7 AP Calculus BC (4 on AP Exam, 5 subscore) Differentiation and integration of single variable functions
8 German III Advanced conversational and written sentence construction; various forms of posessive cases; final year of exhange program with Geschwester-Scholl

Senior Year

Senior year, although cut short by the COVID-19 pandemic, was the year I spent most of my time building my resume and applying to universities. Because I was so ahead in mathematics, the only math courses I was able to take at school were AP Statistics, and a special dual enrollment course in multivariate calculus/linear algebra offered by nearby George Mason University, so I naturally took both courses. I was planning on taking AP Macro/Microeconomics, but because I was taking three advanced courses that were only offered in one period, I was not able to; in fact, I couldn't even take Honors Personal Finance.

Class Period Course Description
1 Multivariable Calculus; Linear Algebra (dual enrolled with George Mason University) Anaylsis of functions of multiple variables; study of parametric fields; topics relating to vectors, matrices. Earned course credit recognized by GMU
2 AP Physics C (opted out of exam) Study of kinematics, graviation, rotation
3 Honors English 12 Analysis of Shakespearean plays, media & advertising; writing college essays
5 AP European History (5 on AP Exam) Study of European history from the Renaissance to present day
6 Economics and Personal Finance Introduction to economics, filing tax reports, investing
7 AP US Government (5); AP Comparative Government (5) Study of the institutions of the US government, as well as the UK, China, Nigeria, Russia, and Iran
8 AP Statistics (4 on AP Exam) Study of distributions, data collection and visualization, probability analysis
